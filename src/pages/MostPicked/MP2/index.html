Carnation

Dianthus caryophyllus

About Plant

The carnation, or Dianthus caryophyllus, is a flower with a rich history and cultural significance. Known for its distinctive ruffled petals and spicy fragrance, carnations come in various colors, including white, red, pink, and yellow. These flowers are often associated with expressions of love, admiration, and affection. Carnations are popular choices for bouquets, arrangements, and even as boutonnieres in weddings and special occasions. Beyond their aesthetic appeal, different colors of carnations convey specific meanings; for example, red carnations symbolize deep love and admiration, while white carnations often represent purity and luck. The versatility and enduring popularity of carnations have made them a timeless favorite in the world of floristry and an enduring symbol of emotion and celebration.
