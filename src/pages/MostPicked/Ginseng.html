Ginseng

Panax ginseng

About Plant

Ginseng, a perennial herb with fleshy roots belonging to the Panax genus, holds a prominent place in traditional medicine and is renowned for its various health benefits. The most widely recognized types are Panax ginseng (Asian ginseng) and Panax quinquefolius (American ginseng). Characterized by its distinctive, forked roots that resemble the human form, ginseng has been used for centuries in Asian cultures as an adaptogen, believed to help the body cope with stress and promote overall well-being. Known for its earthy and slightly bitter taste, ginseng is often consumed as a tea or in dietary supplements. Beyond its traditional uses, ginseng has gained global popularity for its purported ability to enhance mental clarity, boost energy levels, and support immune function. Despite its long history in traditional medicine, it's important to note that individual responses to ginseng can vary, and consulting with healthcare professionals is advisable before incorporating it into one's routine.
